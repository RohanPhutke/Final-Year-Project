# Officer Fall Response — Standard Operating Procedure

## Purpose
This SOP provides guidance for officers responding to fall detection alerts from the automated monitoring system.

## Alert Severity Levels
- **RED (Severe)**: High-energy fall detected, likely injury, immediate response required
- **AMBER (Moderate)**: Fall detected with moderate impact, prompt assessment needed
- **GREEN (Low)**: Minor fall or near-fall, check on subject at next opportunity

## Immediate Response — RED Alerts
1. **Respond within 2 minutes** to the subject's location
2. **Do NOT move the subject** unless they are in immediate danger
3. **Assess consciousness**: speak to the subject, note their response
4. **Call EMS** if any of the following:
   - Subject is unresponsive or confused
   - Visible deformity of any limb
   - Subject reports severe pain
   - Subject cannot stand or bear weight
   - Bleeding that cannot be controlled with direct pressure
   - Subject reports numbness or tingling
5. **Stay with the subject** until EMS arrives
6. **Document**: time of alert, time of response, subject condition, actions taken

## Immediate Response — AMBER Alerts
1. **Respond within 5 minutes**
2. **Assess the subject**: are they in pain? Can they move?
3. **Assist to standing** ONLY if the subject has no pain, no dizziness, and wants to get up
4. **Monitor for 15 minutes** after the fall
5. **Escalate to RED** if condition worsens

## Do NOT
- Move someone with suspected spinal or hip injury
- Give food or drink to a confused or drowsy person
- Leave a RED alert subject unattended
- Reset the alert without physically checking on the subject

## Escalation Matrix
| Condition | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Unresponsive | Call 911/EMS immediately |
| Confused or disoriented | Call EMS, keep subject still |
| Severe pain, cannot move limb | Call EMS, immobilize in position found |
| Minor pain, can mobilize | Assist, monitor, document |
| No injury, fully mobile | Document, reset alert |

## Documentation Required
- Alert ID and timestamp
- Response time (alert to arrival)
- Subject identification
- Observed injuries
- Actions taken
- EMS called? (Y/N, time of call, unit dispatched)
- Disposition (transported / remained on scene / refused care)
