# Cervical Spine — NEXUS & Canadian C-Spine Rule

## NEXUS Low-Risk Criteria
Clear c-spine WITHOUT imaging if ALL five criteria met:
1. No posterior midline cervical tenderness
2. No focal neurological deficit
3. Normal alertness (GCS 15)
4. No intoxication
5. No painful distracting injury

Sensitivity: 99.6%. If ANY criterion fails → image.

## Canadian C-Spine Rule
### Step 1: High-Risk Factor? → Image
- Age ≥ 65
- Dangerous mechanism (fall ≥ 1m / 5 stairs)
- Paresthesias

### Step 2: Low-Risk Factor Allowing ROM?
- Ambulatory, delayed pain onset, no midline tenderness → Step 3

### Step 3: Active Rotation 45° L+R?
- YES → No imaging. NO → Image.

## Imaging Protocol
- **CT cervical spine** is first-line (replaces plain X-rays)
- **MRI**: Add if neurological deficit with normal CT, or obtunded patient

## Key Findings on CT
- Fractures: vertebral body, odontoid (dens), lateral mass
- Alignment: anterior/posterior vertebral lines
- Prevertebral swelling: >7mm at C2, >21mm at C6
- Facet joint widening or perching

## Elderly Considerations
- Odontoid (C2) fractures most common in age >70
- Ankylosing spondylitis: fractures through fused segments
- Anticoagulation: epidural hematoma risk without fracture
