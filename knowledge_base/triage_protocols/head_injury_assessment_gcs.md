# Head Injury Assessment — Glasgow Coma Scale & Triage Protocol

## Overview
Head injuries from falls are a leading cause of traumatic brain injury (TBI). Even low-energy falls (standing height) can cause serious intracranial pathology, especially in elderly patients or those on anticoagulant therapy.

## Glasgow Coma Scale (GCS)

### Eye Opening (E)
| Score | Response |
|-------|----------|
| 4 | Spontaneous |
| 3 | To voice |
| 2 | To pain |
| 1 | None |

### Verbal Response (V)
| Score | Response |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Oriented |
| 4 | Confused |
| 3 | Inappropriate words |
| 2 | Incomprehensible sounds |
| 1 | None |

### Motor Response (M)
| Score | Response |
|-------|----------|
| 6 | Obeys commands |
| 5 | Localizes pain |
| 4 | Withdrawal (flexion) |
| 3 | Abnormal flexion |
| 2 | Extension |
| 1 | None |

### Total GCS Interpretation
- **GCS 13–15**: Mild head injury
- **GCS 9–12**: Moderate head injury
- **GCS 3–8**: Severe head injury (requires intubation)

## Red Flags — Immediate Escalation
- GCS < 13 at any point since injury
- GCS drop of ≥ 2 points
- Loss of consciousness > 5 minutes
- Post-traumatic seizure
- Focal neurological deficit (limb weakness, speech disturbance, pupil asymmetry)
- Suspected skull fracture (Battle sign, raccoon eyes, CSF otorrhea/rhinorrhea)
- Vomiting > 1 episode
- Amnesia > 30 minutes before event
- Coagulopathy or anticoagulant use (warfarin, DOACs)

## Immediate Actions
1. Stabilize the cervical spine — assume c-spine injury until proven otherwise
2. Assess and record GCS every 15 minutes for the first 2 hours
3. Check pupil size and reactivity — asymmetry is a neurosurgical emergency
4. Control any scalp bleeding with direct pressure (scalp wounds bleed heavily)
5. Elevate head of bed/stretcher 30° if no spinal contraindication
6. Do NOT sedate the patient unless absolutely necessary for airway management

## Triage Category
- **RED (Immediate)**: GCS ≤ 8, pupil asymmetry, focal deficit, active deterioration
- **YELLOW (Urgent)**: GCS 9–12, single risk factor, no deterioration
- **GREEN (Delayed)**: GCS 13–15, no risk factors, normal neurological exam

## Transport
- Rapid transport to a facility with CT scanner and neurosurgical capability
- Pre-alert receiving hospital if GCS ≤ 12 or deteriorating
