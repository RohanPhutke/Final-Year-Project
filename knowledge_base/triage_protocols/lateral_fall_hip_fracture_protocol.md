# Lateral Fall — Hip Fracture Triage Protocol

## Overview
Lateral falls (sideways impact) are the leading cause of hip fractures in elderly populations. The greater trochanter absorbs the majority of impact force during lateral falls, and osteoporotic bone dramatically lowers the fracture threshold.

## Immediate Assessment (ABCDE Approach)
1. **Airway**: Ensure airway is patent; jaw thrust if cervical spine concern
2. **Breathing**: Assess respiratory rate, SpO2, chest wall movement
3. **Circulation**: Radial pulse, capillary refill time (<2 seconds normal), skin colour
4. **Disability**: AVPU scale (Alert, Voice, Pain, Unresponsive), pupil reactivity
5. **Exposure**: Carefully expose injured area; check for shortening and external rotation of the affected leg

## Signs of Hip Fracture
- Shortened and externally rotated limb
- Severe pain in the groin, hip, or thigh
- Inability to bear weight or lift the leg
- Tenderness over the greater trochanter
- Bruising over the lateral hip (may develop later)

## Immediate Actions
1. Do NOT attempt to move the patient unless in immediate danger
2. Immobilize the affected limb in the position found
3. Administer analgesia — IV morphine 2–5mg titrated (if authorized) or Entonox
4. Establish IV access — anticipate blood loss (occult hemorrhage common in hip fractures)
5. Keep the patient warm — hypothermia risk is high in elderly ground-level falls
6. Monitor vital signs every 5 minutes

## Triage Category
- **RED (Immediate)**: If hemodynamically unstable, altered consciousness, or open fracture
- **YELLOW (Urgent)**: Closed fracture, hemodynamically stable, significant pain
- **GREEN (Delayed)**: Stable, ambulatory, minimal pain (unlikely for true hip fracture)

## Transport Considerations
- Scoop stretcher preferred — minimizes movement
- Traction splint is CONTRAINDICATED for femoral neck fractures
- Transport in position of comfort, usually supine with padding
- Target hospital with orthopedic surgery capability

## Time-Critical Targets
- Surgery within 36 hours of admission (NICE guideline CG124)
- Mortality increases 10% for every 12-hour delay beyond 48 hours
- Early surgery reduces pressure sore risk, pneumonia, and DVT
