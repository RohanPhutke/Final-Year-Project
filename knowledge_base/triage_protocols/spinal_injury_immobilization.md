# Spinal Injury — Immobilization & Triage Protocol

## Overview
Spinal cord injury (SCI) from falls carries devastating consequences. The primary injury occurs at impact; the goal of prehospital care is to prevent secondary injury through careful immobilization and handling.

## When to Suspect Spinal Injury
- Any fall with head impact or altered consciousness
- Fall from height greater than standing (stairs, ladders)
- Lateral falls with rotational component
- Patient reports neck or back pain
- Neurological symptoms: tingling, numbness, weakness in extremities
- Distracting injury present (e.g., hip fracture) — pain may mask spinal symptoms

## NEXUS Criteria (Low-Risk Rules)
Cervical spine imaging is NOT required if ALL of the following are met:
1. No posterior midline cervical tenderness
2. No focal neurological deficit
3. Normal alertness
4. No intoxication
5. No painful distracting injury

If ANY criterion is failed → cervical spine imaging is required.

## Canadian C-Spine Rule
For alert (GCS 15) and stable trauma patients:
1. Any high-risk factor mandating imaging?
   - Age ≥ 65, dangerous mechanism, paresthesias → YES → Image
2. Any low-risk factor allowing safe ROM assessment?
   - Simple rear-end MVC, sitting in ED, ambulatory, delayed onset neck pain, no midline tenderness → Assess ROM
3. Can the patient actively rotate neck 45° left and right?
   - YES → No imaging needed
   - NO → Image

## Immobilization Technique
1. **Manual In-Line Stabilization (MILS)**: Maintain head/neck in neutral alignment
2. **Cervical collar**: Apply appropriately sized collar (do not over-tighten)
3. **Scoop stretcher**: For lateral transfers — minimizes rolling
4. **Vacuum mattress**: Preferred over long spine board for transport (reduces pressure injuries)
5. **Log roll**: If repositioning is needed, minimum 4 people, coordinated movement

## DO NOT
- Force the neck into neutral if resistance is met
- Use a long spine board for prolonged transport (>30 minutes)
- Remove a helmet without proper technique and assistance
- Allow the patient to sit up or walk if spinal injury is suspected

## Triage Category
- **RED (Immediate)**: Any neurological deficit, respiratory compromise (high cervical injury)
- **YELLOW (Urgent)**: Spinal pain with no deficit, mechanism warrants investigation
- **GREEN (Delayed)**: Cleared by NEXUS/Canadian C-Spine Rule

## Transport
- To nearest Major Trauma Centre (if available)
- Pre-alert: "suspected spinal injury, GCS [X], neurological status [intact/deficit]"
- Monitor for neurogenic shock: hypotension + bradycardia (loss of sympathetic tone)
