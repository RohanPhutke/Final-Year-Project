# Fall Response Checklist — EMS Protocol

## Dispatch Information to Gather
- Caller relationship to patient
- Patient age, sex, known medical conditions
- Mechanism: witnessed vs unwitnessed fall
- Time of fall and time on ground (long-lie?)
- Current consciousness level
- Visible injuries or deformity
- Any seizure activity

## On-Scene Primary Assessment
1. Scene safety — hazards, trip risks
2. C-spine precautions if mechanism warrants
3. ABCDE assessment
4. GCS scoring
5. Blood glucose (hypoglycemia is a common fall cause)
6. 12-lead ECG if syncope suspected
7. Temperature (hypothermia if long-lie)

## Secondary Survey
- Head-to-toe examination
- Document ALL injuries found
- Assess pain using numeric scale (0–10)
- Check medication list — anticoagulants, antihypertensives, sedatives
- Assess pre-fall mobility status

## Key Measurements to Record
- Heart rate, blood pressure, respiratory rate, SpO2, temperature
- GCS (E+V+M breakdown)
- Blood glucose
- Pupil size and reactivity (PEARRL)
- Pain score

## Decision Points
### Transport Required (ANY of the following):
- Suspected fracture (hip, spine, wrist, skull)
- GCS < 15 or any neurological deficit
- Hemodynamic instability
- Anticoagulated patient with head injury
- Unable to mobilize safely
- Syncope as cause of fall

### Consider Non-Transport (ALL must be met):
- Minor soft tissue injury only
- GCS 15, fully oriented
- Able to mobilize independently
- No anticoagulation with head injury
- Safe home environment
- Follow-up plan in place
